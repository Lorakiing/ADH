A Doll's House

By Henrik Ibsen

In Henrik Ibsen's groundbreaking play "A Doll's House" (1879), Nora Helmer lives what appears to be a perfect life with her husband Torvald and their three children. However, a secret debt from her past threatens their marriage when Torvald discovers Nora forged her father's signature to secure a loan that saved his life. The play culminates in Nora's dramatic awakening to the realization that she's been living as a "doll" in her own home, leading to a revolutionary decision that shocked audiences of the time.
